# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

High-level executives, founders, and senior professionals (≈35–60) across Pasadena and Greater LA who know AI matters and want to *get* it — not another webinar. Their context when they hit this page: skeptical, time-poor, design-literate, deciding whether a 2-day in-person workshop is worth their money and two days out of the office. The job to be done: feel confident this is credible, ambitious, and for people like them — then reserve a seat (or apply for a call).

## Product Purpose

A marketing site for the Pasadena AI Institute — a 2-day, in-person, hands-on AI workshop hosted by Whistle Labs. The site itself is the primary sales asset and a proof of craft: it has to demonstrate, on sight, that beauty and AI belong together. Success = a beautifully art-directed site that makes an executive feel the workshop is exceptional and reserve a seat.

## Brand Personality

Cultured, generative, luminous. The voice of a great gallery curator who also ships software: confident, warm, unhurried, never hype. It should feel like walking into a light-filled gallery of impressionist masterworks — invited to look closely, then to create. Emotional goal: aesthetic awe → belonging → the urge to build.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS / dev-tool landing pages (Linear/Vercel clones, dark dashboards, terminal cosplay).
- **AI slop** as defined by the installed impeccable detector: purple/amber gradients, glassmorphism, neon glow orbs, gradient text, animate-everything, side-stripe cards, identical icon-card grids, 01/02/03 markers, tiny tracked eyebrows on every section, massive decorative icons.
- The warm-cream / sand "editorial restraint" template and the display-serif-italic + mono-label editorial-magazine lane (both now saturated AI tells).
- Coding-bootcamp energy. This is not a code school; it's a creative atelier.

## Design Principles

1. **The site is the proof.** Its beauty is the argument. If it doesn't make you feel something, it failed the brief.
2. **Art over interface.** Real paintings carry the color, depth, and emotion. The UI chrome stays museum-quiet so the work sings.
3. **A gallery, not a dashboard.** Generous space, deliberate pacing, one dominant idea per fold. Walls, not widgets.
4. **Invite creation.** Every page nudges toward "come make something here," not "buy this product."
5. **Restraint is the luxury.** Confidence shown by what we leave out. No decoration that isn't the art itself.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Text over imagery always sits on a scrim/overlay that guarantees ≥4.5:1 (≥3:1 for large display). Full keyboard paths and visible focus. Every motion (parallax, reveals, Ken Burns) has a `prefers-reduced-motion` fallback. Body text ≥16px, zoomable to 200%.
